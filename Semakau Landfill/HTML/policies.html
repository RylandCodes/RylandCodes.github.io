Government Policies in Action


Singapore's Approach to Landfill Waste Management

The Singapore government has implemented several policies to address the growing waste problem and extend the lifespan of Semakau Landfill. One of the most significant initiatives is the Zero Waste Masterplan, which aims to reduce the amount of waste sent to landfills by 30% by 2030. This policy encourages waste reduction, reuse, and recycling, shifting towards a circular economy.

In addition, the government has introduced the Waste-to-Energy (WTE) program, which helps reduce the volume of waste sent to Semakau Landfill. WTE plants incinerate non-recyclable waste, converting it into energy. The government also promotes extended producer responsibility, holding manufacturers accountable for the waste produced by their products, and encouraging them to design with sustainability in mind.
