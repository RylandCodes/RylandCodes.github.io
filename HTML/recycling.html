Recycling Efforts to Tackle Semakau Landfill

8 February 2025

Government Recycling Policies

The Singapore government has implemented several key initiatives to increase recycling rates and reduce waste. One of the most prominent strategies is the Zero Waste Masterplan, which aims to reduce waste sent to the landfill by 30% by 2030. This initiative emphasizes reducing, reusing, and recycling as central pillars to waste management in Singapore.

Waste Segregation and Public Engagement

Public awareness and education are key to Singapore's success in improving recycling. The government has launched programs to teach citizens about waste segregation. The 3R (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle) campaign encourages households and businesses to separate recyclable materials from general waste. Recycling bins are made available in public spaces, and the government is investing in public education campaigns to foster a more sustainable mindset among the population.

Recycling Infrastructure

Singapore has invested in state-of-the-art recycling infrastructure. The Singapore Recycling Programme aims to expand curbside recycling services to all residents. Singapore’s waste management company, Sembcorp (formerly known as SembWaste), is responsible for operating recycling plants across the island that process materials like paper, plastic, metal, and glass. These recycling plants help divert waste from the landfill and process recyclable materials into reusable resources for the economy.

Waste-to-Energy Solutions

In addition to recycling, Singapore has developed advanced waste-to-energy technologies to reduce the volume of waste that ends up in Semakau Landfill. The country’s Waste-to-Energy (WTE) plants incinerate non-recyclable waste to generate electricity. This technology not only reduces waste volume but also contributes to Singapore's energy needs. Furthermore, the Tuas Nexus integrated waste management facility combines WTE plants with water treatment facilities, significantly improving overall waste processing efficiency.



Waste-to-Energy Facilities in Singapore


Recycling Challenges

Despite the government’s efforts, challenges remain in achieving higher recycling rates. Singapore still faces a significant gap between the amount of waste generated and the amount that is effectively recycled. Common issues include contamination of recyclable materials, lack of public awareness, and difficulties in recycling certain materials like plastic. The government is continuing to invest in research and innovation to tackle these challenges and improve recycling technologies.
