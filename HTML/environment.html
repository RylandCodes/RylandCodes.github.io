Environmental Issues of Semakau Landfill

8 February 2025

The Environmental Impact of Waste Accumulation

As waste accumulates, the environmental impacts of Semakau Landfill become more pronounced. The landfill contributes to environmental degradation in several ways, affecting both the land and the surrounding waters.

Air Pollution and Greenhouse Gas Emissions

As waste decomposes in the landfill, methane gas is produced—a potent greenhouse gas that contributes to climate change. Semakau Landfill is equipped with a gas collection system to capture and flare methane, thereby reducing its release into the atmosphere. This system helps mitigate uncontrolled emissions, aiming to protect air quality and combat global warming.

Contamination of Groundwater and Marine Ecosystems

Leachate, a toxic liquid formed when rainwater filters through waste, poses a serious environmental threat. To prevent leachate from contaminating local water supplies, including underground aquifers and nearby seas, Semakau Landfill is designed with a 7 km perimeter rock bund lined with impermeable membranes and marine clay. This design ensures that leachate is contained within the landfill, protecting marine life and preserving biodiversity in the area.

Loss of Biodiversity

The accumulation of waste and the environmental contamination caused by Semakau Landfill endanger marine and land-based ecosystems. However, the landfill’s design incorporates measures to protect the surrounding environment. For instance, the bund’s impermeable lining prevents leachate from seeping into the sea, thereby safeguarding marine biodiversity.

The Challenge of Expanding Waste Disposal Capacity

Singapore’s limited land space means that if Semakau Landfill fills up, the country will face significant challenges in finding new land to build additional landfills. This would impact housing, infrastructure, and green spaces, contributing to the country's ongoing space constraints.

In addition to land constraints, a landfill on mainland Singapore would exacerbate air pollution, contamination of water sources, and even contribute to climate change. Therefore, it is essential to focus on better waste management solutions.

Solutions to Minimize Environmental Damage

To address the environmental challenges posed by Semakau Landfill, the Singapore government has launched the Zero Waste Masterplan, aiming to reduce the daily waste sent to the landfill by 30% by 2030. This initiative is crucial to extending the landfill's lifespan and mitigate its environmental impact. Additionally, the National Environment Agency (NEA) is exploring innovative solutions such as processing landfilled incineration ash for use in reclamation projects like the Tuas Port Phase 3. These measures, along with public participation in recycling and reducing waste, are essential to minimize environmental damage and promote sustainability in Singapore’s waste management system.
